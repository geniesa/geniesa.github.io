What I can do.

I've been working on content, communications, and marketing-related stuff for the last decade. I like avoiding empty rhreotric and making things as simple — and sustainable — as possible.

My preferences tend to lean in that general direction of not-making-society-more-crappy, and I’m privileged enough to be able to continue taking on projects of that nature. I like solving problems and think that a job well done involves an original state of a thing being improved, clarified, and evolved into something unexpectedly pleasant.

No, I did not forget about me promising pictures of koalas.

Koalas are kinda jerks though. I mean, I’m not really going to spend a bunch of space bagging on koalas.

Reasons why koalas are stupid jerks:
  • You can’t expect to get any respect if you sleep 18-22 hours a day, unlike giraffes. SORRY NOT SORRY, KOALAS.
  • They have THE WORST dierary requirements, eating only eucalyptus (UNSUSTAINABLE!)... which is poisonous in large amounts. These plants are super oily, hard to eat, and have a natural sedative effect (HOW CONVENIENT!). Their digestive systems have somehow failed at this whole adaptation thing, so koalas remain TOO SENSITIVE to do anything else while their bodies are breaking this stuff down. So they keep crashing like over-tired Sims.
  • Baby koalas (CUTE? MAYBE.) don’t have the correct sort of awkward gut bacteria to break down eucalyptus, and therefore, the natural way of gaining this is OBVIOUSLY to EAT THEIR MOTHERS’ POOP. It’s not even just any old poop, but some leaky runny variant that involves acquisition via some nuzzling action.
  • When adult koalas can’t digest their eucalyptus, their logical solution is to regurgitate whatever’s in their mouths so they can chew it all over again (SUSTAINABLE, I GUESS!).
  • They STILL only eat eucalyptus. ONLY eucalyptus.
  • When they ARE awake, they’re eating more eucalyptus.
  • They make loud chewy noises when they’re eating said eucalyptus.
  • They apparently either smell like eucalyptus, even stronger-smelling eucalyptus, or cough drops.
  • They appear to communicate by screaming demonically.
  • They also spend quite a lot of their 2-4 waking hours a day raping each other.
  • There is enough koala chlamydia to possibly wipe out entire populations.
  • That koala chlamydia can be transmitted to us humans via koala pee.
  • They have smooth, singular lumps for brains because eucalyptus doesn’t really contain nutrients.
  • Their brains are also so tiny and smooth that they’re unable to perform tasks as PAINFULLY COMPLEX as eating their precious eucalyptus leaves from a flat surface.

Anyway, I hope that was at least a mildly entertaining diversion and that you now no longer want any pictures of koalas from me. For the record, I’m not genuinely passionate about having an anti-koala stance, but maybe now you’ll know what sort of content belongs on a high-quality webpage.

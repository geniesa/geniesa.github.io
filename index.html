Leave me with your out-of-the-box projects. And also the projects inside the box waiting to be released via some form of creative coaxing, Scooby Doo voodoo shenanigans, or good, brutal editing. I'll find some way to make anything fun (at least for myself) somehow.

No, I did not forget about me promising pictures of koalas.

Koalas are kind of stupid jerks though. I mean, I'm not really going to spend all this space bagging on koalas.

Reasons why koalas are stupid jerks:
  • You can't expect to get any respect if you sleep 18-22 hours a day, unlike giraffes. Sorry, koalas. That's just not good empathy material.
  • They have worse dietary requirements than anyone you'll ever meet, eating only eucalyptus, which are poisonous in large amounts. These plants are super oily, hard to eat, and have a natural sedative effect. Koalas' digestive systems have somehow failed at this whole adaptation thing (aside for the 'only eats one thing to live' part), and remain TOO SENSITIVE to do anything else while breaking this stuff down, so they keep crashing like over-tired Sims.
  • Baby koalas don't have this awkward gut bacteria that breaks down eucalyptus, and therefore, the natural way of gaining this is OBVIOUSLY to EAT THEIR MOTHERS' POOP. It's not even just any old poop, but some leaky runny variant that involves acquisition via some nuzzling action.
  • When adult koalas can't digest their eucalyptus, their logical solution is to regurgitate whatever's in their mouths so they can chew it all over again. Classy.
  • AND YET, they eat eucalyptus and ONLY eucalyptus in spite of this whole toxicity thing, AND the fact that this makes them unproductive members of society.
  • When they ARE awake, they're eating more eucalyptus.
  • They make loud chewy noises when they're eating said eucalyptus.
  • They apparently either smell like eucalyptus, even stronger-smelling eucalyptus, or cough drops.
  • They appear to communicate by screaming demonically.
  • They also spend quite a lot of their 2-4 waking hours a day raping each other.
  • Koala chlamydia. Lots of it. Enough koala chlamydia to possibly wipe out entire populations.
  • That koala chlamydia can be transmitted to us humans via koala pee.
  • They have smooth, singular lumps for brains because eucalyptus doesn't really contain nutrients.
  • They also have extremely tiny brains.
  • Their brains are so tiny and smooth that they're unable to perform tasks as COMPLEX as eating their precious eucalyptus leaves from a flat surface.

Anyway, I hope that was at least a mildly entertaining diversion and you now no longer want any pictures of koalas from me. For the record, I'm not inherently passionate about having an anti-koala stance — don't even get me started on how stupid kiwi are. Just keep voting for the kea, kererū, or kakapo as Bird of the Year.

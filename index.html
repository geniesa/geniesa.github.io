Auckland Programme for Space Systems

I was part of a team of three people scraping our way through this brand new initiative that a guy just made up in 2016. There are many horrifying anecdotes including how we came up with the name, how we publicised all this stuff internally with no money, and basically a lot of making it up as we go along. I was the self-proclaimed communications director and alogside making up the entire programme's lineup of printed stuff with zero turnover time, I also designed, built, and wrote copy for the APSS website.

This programme is (miraculously) still going strong. It attracts over 200 undergraduates each semester who participate for no academic credit. Students went off to create their own startups. It also became the thing that throttled the university's involvement in the nationwide space agency. They now offer several postgraduate study options in aerospace engineering (which I had to write copy for). We also launched country’s first-ever university satellite into space in November 2020.

Engineering course viewer

This one's a strange project. It was one of the final things I've done before leaving faculty, so time was a constraint. There has been a demand for digital course outlines for years. While the university has finally provided that in early 2021, it still didn't fulfill the needs of users and those guiding them in person, who'd usually show them a list of tables full of courses they can and cannot take.

The people wanted their tables and exact information, so I assembled a team of two academics. One guy provided the admin and user need, the other coded the thing that dragged information out of a bunch of spreadsheets in Python over a weekend. I taught a software engineering lecturer BootStrap and we threw the course viewer together in two weeks. It isn't super pretty, but it works.

Print audits and refreshes.

Overhauling legacy publications at a large, unchanging institution was no easy feat. A lot of it involves changing tradition – from which (usually underrepresented) faces and voices you feature, to overall strategy and messaging. The following are some things where I've been involved with at every stage of the production: concept, copy, design, and getting them printed.

Undergraduate flyers

The state of undergraduate promotional publications in 2015 or so was a mess for a faculty meant to show off a consistent front. Each department kinda made up their own variants, so the copy, design, and formats looked really different. Many of these undergraduate flyers were double-sided A4 things folded into three, sometimes printed on normal paper in house.

My job was to take over and figure out what to do with each one: five departments and nine specialisations. It took a lot of time planning, writing, and prioritising what individual people with their individual opinions wanted to tell their audience. I ended up creating a template for everybody, and we stuck to a single format to keep things looking unified. We got to reuse a bunch of photoshoots and some fun textural shots that always didn't have a purpose until now. People liked the result. Other marketing folks were impressed and wanted to copy them. That was a good result. PDF of the befores and afters.

No, I did not forget about me promising pictures of koalas.

Koalas are kinda jerks though. I mean, I'm not really going to spend a bunch of space bagging on koalas.

Reasons why koalas are stupid jerks:
  • You can't expect to get any respect if you sleep 18-22 hours a day, unlike giraffes. SORRY NOT SORRY, KOALAS.
  • They have THE WORST dierary requirements, eating only eucalyptus (UNSUSTAINABLE!)... which are poisonous in large amounts. These plants are super oily, hard to eat, and have a natural sedative effect (HOW CONVENIENT!). Their digestive systems have somehow failed at this whole adaptation thing, and remain TOO SENSITIVE to do anything else while breaking this stuff down, so they keep crashing like over-tired Sims.
  • Baby koalas (CUTE? MAYBE.) don't have the right awkward gut bacteria to break down eucalyptus, and therefore, the natural way of gaining this is OBVIOUSLY to EAT THEIR MOTHERS' POOP. It's not even just any old poop, but some leaky runny variant that involves acquisition via some nuzzling action.
  • When adult koalas can't digest their eucalyptus, their logical solution is to regurgitate whatever's in their mouths so they can chew it all over again (SUSTAINABLE, I GUESS!).
  • They STILL only eat eucalyptus. ONLY eucalyptus.
  • When they ARE awake, they're eating more eucalyptus.
  • They make loud chewy noises when they're eating said eucalyptus.
  • They apparently either smell like eucalyptus, even stronger-smelling eucalyptus, or cough drops.
  • They appear to communicate by screaming demonically.
  • They also spend quite a lot of their 2-4 waking hours a day raping each other.
  • There is enough koala chlamydia to possibly wipe out entire populations.
  • That koala chlamydia can be transmitted to us humans via koala pee.
  • They have smooth, singular lumps for brains because eucalyptus doesn't really contain nutrients.
  • Their brains are also so tiny and smooth that they're unable to perform tasks as PAINFULLY COMPLEX as eating their precious eucalyptus leaves from a flat surface.

Anyway, I hope that was at least a mildly entertaining diversion and that you now no longer want any pictures of koalas from me. For the record, I'm not genuinely passionate about having an anti-koala stance, but maybe now you'll know what sort of content belongs on a high-quality webpage.
